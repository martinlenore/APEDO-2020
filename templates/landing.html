Peptide Database

Our objective is to provide valuable information about antimicrobial peptides in one place. Amino acid sequences are the most unambiguous way to characterize peptides with diverse naming conventions.

Microbe Database

Peptides that kill microbes are tested by many different methods, such as in vivo, or in broth, or as inhibition zones on plates, and antimicrobial activity concentration units must be clearly defined as moles/L or g/L.

Structure Information

Secondary and tertiary structural information is largely derived from the RCSB database and is classified according to the method used, such as X-ray, NMR, and Cryot-TEM methods.

Why AMPed?

The idea behind "AMPed" (Anti-Microbial Peptide Editable Database) is to create an annotated collection of antimicrobial peptides that are sourced from several online repositories, journals and existing large databases such as ATCC, PDB, UniProt and NCBI with the purpose of uniformity and coherence.
